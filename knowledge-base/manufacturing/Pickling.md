# Pickling

## Overview

Pickling is a surface treatment process performed after Hot Rolling to remove oxide scale, rust, and other surface impurities formed during high-temperature processing. The process uses controlled acid solutions to clean the steel surface without affecting the base material.

At Jindal Stainless Limited (JSL), pickling plays an essential role in preparing stainless steel for Cold Rolling and other downstream finishing operations. Proper pickling improves surface quality, corrosion resistance, and product appearance.

# Why This Process is Important

During Hot Rolling, the steel surface reacts with oxygen at high temperatures, forming an oxide layer known as mill scale.

Pickling removes this layer to:

- Improve surface finish.
- Enhance corrosion resistance.
- Prepare material for Cold Rolling.
- Improve coating and finishing quality.
- Meet customer surface quality requirements.

Without proper pickling, surface defects may affect both product quality and downstream manufacturing.

---

# Working Principle

The Pickling Line passes hot rolled coils through a sequence of acid tanks where chemical reactions dissolve the oxide scale from the steel surface.

After acid treatment, the material is thoroughly washed, neutralized, dried, and recoiled for further processing.

The process is carefully monitored to ensure complete cleaning without damaging the stainless steel.

---

# Major Components

A typical Pickling Line consists of:

- Entry Section
- Uncoiler
- Scale Breaker (if applicable)
- Acid Tanks
- Rinsing Tanks
- Neutralization Section
- Drying Unit
- Inspection Section
- Recoiler
- Process Control System

Each component contributes to achieving a clean, defect-free surface.

---

# Input Material

The Pickling Line receives:

- Hot Rolled Stainless Steel Coils
- Pickling Acid Solution
- Process Water
- Neutralizing Chemicals

---

# Process Flow

The Pickling process generally follows these stages:

1. Hot Rolled Coil is loaded.
2. Coil enters the Pickling Line.
3. Surface scale is chemically removed.
4. Material is rinsed with clean water.
5. Neutralization is performed.
6. Strip is dried.
7. Surface inspection is carried out.
8. Coil is recoiled.
9. Material is transferred to the Cold Rolling Mill.

---

# Acid Treatment

The acid treatment stage removes:

- Oxide Scale
- Surface Rust
- Heat Tint
- Metallic Contaminants

Process parameters such as acid concentration, temperature, and exposure time are continuously monitored to ensure consistent surface quality.

---

# Rinsing and Neutralization

After pickling:

- Residual acid is removed using water.
- Neutralization prevents corrosion.
- The strip is cleaned before drying.
- Surface cleanliness is verified.

---

# Drying Process

The strip passes through drying equipment to remove moisture before recoiling.

Proper drying prevents:

- Surface staining
- Flash rust
- Water marks
- Contamination during storage

---

# Output

The Pickling process produces:

- Clean Stainless Steel Coils
- Oxide-Free Surface
- Material Ready for Cold Rolling

---

# Quality Control

Quality assurance activities include:

- Surface cleanliness inspection
- Acid concentration monitoring
- Temperature monitoring
- Strip thickness verification
- Surface defect inspection
- Coil identification
- Traceability verification

These checks ensure that only approved material proceeds to the next manufacturing stage.

---

# Safety Considerations

Pickling involves chemical handling and corrosive acids.

Personnel should:

- Wear acid-resistant PPE.
- Follow chemical handling procedures.
- Use eye and face protection.
- Monitor ventilation systems.
- Handle acids according to plant safety guidelines.
- Follow emergency spill response procedures.
- Report leaks immediately.

Strict adherence to chemical safety procedures is mandatory.

---

# Common Operational Challenges

Typical challenges include:

- Incomplete scale removal.
- Over-pickling.
- Acid contamination.
- Surface staining.
- Chemical concentration imbalance.
- Strip damage.
- Equipment corrosion.

Proper monitoring and preventive maintenance help minimize these issues.

---

# Best Practices

JSL follows several best practices during Pickling:

- Regular acid quality monitoring.
- Controlled process parameters.
- Continuous strip inspection.
- Effective rinsing.
- Proper waste treatment.
- Scheduled equipment maintenance.
- Accurate process documentation.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Pickling removes oxide scale formed during Hot Rolling.
- Chemical treatment improves surface quality.
- Proper rinsing and neutralization are essential.
- The process prepares material for Cold Rolling.
- Surface quality directly affects the final product.
