# Raw Material Procurement


## Overview

Raw Material Procurement is the first stage of the stainless steel manufacturing process. It involves sourcing, inspecting, storing, and preparing the raw materials required for steel production. High-quality raw materials are essential to ensure consistent product quality, efficient production, and cost-effective operations.

At Jindal Stainless Limited (JSL), procurement follows strict quality standards to ensure that only approved materials enter the manufacturing process.

# Why This Process is Important

The quality of stainless steel depends heavily on the quality of the raw materials used.

Poor-quality raw materials may lead to:

- Chemical composition defects
- Mechanical property failures
- Increased production costs
- Equipment damage
- Product rejection

An effective procurement process ensures:

- Consistent material quality
- Reliable production schedules
- Reduced manufacturing costs
- Compliance with industry standards
- Improved customer satisfaction

---

# Raw Materials Used

The primary raw materials used during stainless steel manufacturing include:

- Stainless steel scrap
- Carbon steel scrap
- Ferro Chromium (FeCr)
- Ferro Nickel (FeNi)
- Ferro Molybdenum (FeMo)
- Nickel
- Chromium
- Lime
- Dolomite
- Flux materials
- Alloying elements

The exact composition varies depending on the stainless steel grade being produced.

---

# Procurement Workflow

The procurement process generally consists of the following stages:

1. Production Planning identifies material requirements.
2. Purchase Requisition is generated.
3. Approved suppliers are selected.
4. Purchase Orders are issued.
5. Materials are delivered to the plant.
6. Incoming material inspection is performed.
7. Quality approval is obtained.
8. Materials are stored in designated locations.
9. Approved materials are issued to production.

---

# Material Inspection

Every incoming material undergoes quality verification before acceptance.

Typical inspection activities include:

- Visual inspection
- Weight verification
- Chemical composition testing
- Material grade verification
- Moisture inspection
- Contamination inspection
- Supplier documentation verification

Materials failing inspection are either rejected or quarantined for further evaluation.

---

# Storage and Inventory

After approval, raw materials are stored according to predefined storage procedures.

Storage practices include:

- Separate storage based on material type
- Protection from contamination
- Inventory labeling
- Batch traceability
- FIFO (First In, First Out) management
- Environmental protection where applicable

Proper storage ensures material quality is maintained until production.

# Quality Control Measures

Quality assurance focuses on:

- Approved supplier evaluation
- Incoming quality inspection
- Laboratory analysis
- Batch traceability
- Material certification
- Inventory monitoring

These measures reduce production risks and maintain product consistency.

---

# Safety Considerations

Personnel involved in procurement and material handling should follow all plant safety guidelines.

Important safety practices include:

- Wear mandatory PPE.
- Follow lifting and crane safety procedures.
- Inspect handling equipment before use.
- Maintain safe storage conditions.
- Prevent mixing of incompatible materials.
- Report damaged materials immediately.

---

# Common Challenges

Some common issues during procurement include:

- Delayed supplier deliveries
- Incorrect material grades
- Material contamination
- Inventory shortages
- Documentation errors
- Supplier quality variations

Proper planning and supplier management help minimize these risks.

JSL follows several best practices to improve procurement efficiency:

- Quality-first acceptance policy
- Continuous inventory monitoring

# Key Takeaways

- Procurement is the starting point of stainless steel manufacturing.
- High-quality raw materials are essential for producing quality steel.
- Every material undergoes inspection before entering production.
- Proper storage and traceability improve manufacturing efficiency.
