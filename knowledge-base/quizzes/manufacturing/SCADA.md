# Question 1

### What does SCADA stand for?

- A. System Control and Data Automation
- B. Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition
- C. Smart Control and Data Analysis
- D. Supervisory Communication and Digital Access

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

SCADA stands for Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition. It is used to monitor, supervise, and control industrial processes.

---

# Question 2

### What is the primary purpose of a SCADA system?

- A. Melt stainless steel
- B. Monitor and supervise industrial processes
- C. Manufacture sensors
- D. Package finished products

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

SCADA provides operators with real-time information and supervisory control over industrial operations.

# Question 3

### Which device usually provides data to a SCADA system?

- A. PLC
- B. Calculator
- C. Printer
- D. Mobile Phone

**Correct Answer:** A

**Explanation:**

SCADA collects process information from PLCs and displays it through graphical interfaces.

# Question 4

### Which interface allows operators to monitor plant operations visually?

- A. GPS
- B. HMI (Human Machine Interface)
- C. Keyboard
- D. Barcode Scanner

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

# Question 6

### What is the purpose of alarms in a SCADA system?

- A. Entertain operators
- B. Notify operators of abnormal conditions
- C. Increase machine speed
- D. Reduce production

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

SCADA alarms help operators respond quickly to faults and prevent equipment damage.

# Question 9

### During plant operation, an alarm indicates that furnace temperature has exceeded the safe limit. What should the operator do first?

- A. Ignore the alarm
- B. Verify the alarm and investigate the process condition
- C. Shut down the entire factory immediately
- D. Restart the computer without checking the process

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Operators should first verify the alarm and determine its cause before taking corrective action according to plant procedures.

# Question 10

### Which statement best describes the relationship between PLC and SCADA?

- A. SCADA replaces the PLC
- B. PLC controls the process while SCADA monitors and supervises it
- C. PLC and SCADA perform exactly the same function
- D. SCADA only stores employee information

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

The PLC performs machine control, while SCADA provides visualization, monitoring, reporting, and supervisory control.
