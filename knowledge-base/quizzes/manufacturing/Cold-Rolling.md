# Question 1

### What is the primary purpose of the Cold Rolling process?

- A. To melt stainless steel
- B. To reduce thickness and improve surface finish
- C. To package finished products
- D. To remove oxide scale

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Cold Rolling reduces the thickness of stainless steel while improving dimensional accuracy, strength, and surface finish.

---

# Question 2

### At what temperature is Cold Rolling performed?

- A. Above 1500°C
- B. Above the recrystallization temperature
- C. Room temperature or below the recrystallization temperature
- D. Inside a furnace

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:**

Unlike Hot Rolling, Cold Rolling is performed below the recrystallization temperature.

---

# Question 3

### Which process usually takes place before Cold Rolling?

- A. Dispatch
- B. Pickling
- C. Grinding
- D. Warehouse Storage

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Pickling removes oxide scale before the steel enters the Cold Rolling Mill.

---

# Question 4

### Which property improves significantly after Cold Rolling?

- A. Surface Finish
- B. Dimensional Accuracy
- C. Strength
- D. All of the Above

**Correct Answer:** D

**Explanation:**

Cold Rolling improves surface finish, dimensional accuracy, and mechanical strength.

---

# Question 5

### Which equipment performs the Cold Rolling process?

- A. Electric Arc Furnace
- B. Cold Rolling Mill
- C. Pickling Tank
- D. Annealing Furnace

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

A Cold Rolling Mill uses rollers to reduce the thickness of steel at room temperature.

---

# Question 6

### Why is lubrication used during Cold Rolling?

- A. To increase friction
- B. To reduce friction and improve surface quality
- C. To increase steel temperature
- D. To remove rust

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Lubrication minimizes friction, reduces wear on rollers, and improves product quality.

---

# Question 7

### Which parameter is continuously monitored during Cold Rolling?

- A. Strip Thickness
- B. Rolling Speed
- C. Roll Pressure
- D. All of the Above

**Correct Answer:** D

**Explanation:**

Maintaining proper thickness, speed, and pressure ensures consistent product quality.

---

# Question 8

### Which process generally follows Cold Rolling?

- A. Annealing
- B. Raw Material Procurement
- C. Dispatch
- D. Scrap Management

**Correct Answer:** A

**Explanation:**

Cold-worked steel is annealed to restore ductility and relieve internal stresses.

---

# Question 9

### Why is quality inspection important after Cold Rolling?

- A. To verify thickness and surface quality
- B. To increase warehouse space
- C. To prepare invoices
- D. To package the product

**Correct Answer:** A

**Explanation:**

Inspection ensures the product meets customer specifications before further processing.

---

# Question 10

### Which of the following is an advantage of Cold Rolling?

- A. Better dimensional accuracy
- B. Improved surface finish
- C. Increased mechanical strength
- D. All of the Above

**Correct Answer:** D

**Explanation:**

Cold Rolling provides better precision, smoother surfaces, and improved mechanical properties.
