# Question 1

### What is the primary objective of the steel making process?

- A. Package finished products
- B. Convert raw materials into molten stainless steel
- C. Store finished coils
- D. Paint steel sheets

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Steel making converts raw materials such as stainless steel scrap and alloying elements into molten stainless steel with the desired chemical composition.

---

# Question 2

### Which process is responsible for melting stainless steel scrap?

- A. Cold Rolling
- B. Electric Arc Furnace (EAF)
- C. Grinding
- D. Dispatch

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

The Electric Arc Furnace uses electrical energy to melt stainless steel scrap before refining.

---

# Question 3

### Which process refines molten steel after melting?

- A. Hot Rolling
- B. Pickling
- C. Argon Oxygen Decarburization (AOD)
- D. Warehouse

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:**

The AOD process reduces carbon while preserving chromium and other alloying elements.

---

# Question 4

### Which alloying element is mainly responsible for the corrosion resistance of stainless steel?

- A. Carbon
- B. Chromium
- C. Copper
- D. Aluminum

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Chromium forms a protective oxide layer that gives stainless steel its corrosion resistance.

---

# Question 5

### Why are alloying elements added during steel making?

- A. To improve mechanical and chemical properties
- B. To reduce warehouse space
- C. To package products
- D. To reduce product weight

**Correct Answer:** A

**Explanation:**

Different alloying elements improve strength, hardness, corrosion resistance, and other material properties.

---

# Question 6

### Which parameter is continuously monitored during steel making?

- A. Temperature
- B. Chemical Composition
- C. Carbon Content
- D. All of the Above

**Correct Answer:** D

**Explanation:**

Temperature and chemical composition are carefully controlled to achieve the required steel grade.

---

# Question 7

### Why is slag important during steel making?

- A. It increases product weight
- B. It removes impurities from molten steel
- C. It cools the furnace
- D. It transports steel

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Slag absorbs impurities and protects molten steel during refining.

---

# Question 8

### Which process generally follows steel making?

- A. Continuous Casting
- B. Warehouse Storage
- C. Sales
- D. Dispatch

**Correct Answer:** A

**Explanation:**

After refining, molten steel is transferred to the Continuous Casting Machine to form slabs, blooms, or billets.

---

# Question 9

### Why is automation important in steel making?

- A. To monitor process parameters accurately
- B. To improve safety
- C. To improve consistency
- D. All of the Above

**Correct Answer:** D

**Explanation:**

Automation improves process control, safety, consistency, and production efficiency.

---

# Question 10

### What is the most important outcome of an effective steel making process?

- A. Decorative packaging
- B. High-quality molten stainless steel
- C. Warehouse expansion
- D. Faster transportation

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

The goal of steel making is to produce molten stainless steel with the required quality and composition for further processing.
