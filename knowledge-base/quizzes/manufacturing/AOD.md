# Question 1

### What does AOD stand for?

- A. Automatic Oxygen Distribution
- B. Argon Oxygen Decarburization
- C. Air Oxygen Distribution
- D. Automatic Operation Department

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

AOD is a refining process used in stainless steel manufacturing to reduce carbon while preserving valuable alloying elements.

---

# Question 2

### Which gas is primarily used along with oxygen during the AOD process?

- A. Nitrogen
- B. Hydrogen
- C. Argon
- D. Helium

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:**

Argon is injected with oxygen to control oxidation and improve the efficiency of decarburization.

---

# Question 3

### What is the main objective of the AOD process?

- A. Cooling the steel
- B. Packaging steel
- C. Reducing carbon content
- D. Grinding the surface

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:**

The primary purpose of AOD is to reduce carbon without significantly oxidizing chromium.

---

# Question 4

### Which alloying element is protected during AOD refining?

- A. Copper
- B. Chromium
- C. Zinc
- D. Aluminum

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Chromium provides corrosion resistance and must be preserved during refining.

---

# Question 5

### After which manufacturing process does AOD occur?

- A. Pickling
- B. Electric Arc Furnace
- C. Grinding
- D. Dispatch

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Molten steel from the Electric Arc Furnace is transferred to the AOD converter for refining.

---

# Question 6

### Which parameter is carefully monitored during AOD?

- A. Employee Attendance
- B. Carbon Content
- C. Office Temperature
- D. Warehouse Capacity

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Carbon levels are continuously monitored to achieve the required steel composition.

---

# Question 7

### Why is oxygen injected into the molten steel?

- A. To cool the steel
- B. To remove carbon
- C. To increase weight
- D. To transport steel

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Oxygen reacts with carbon to form gases that leave the molten steel, reducing carbon concentration.

- C. Warehouse Crane
