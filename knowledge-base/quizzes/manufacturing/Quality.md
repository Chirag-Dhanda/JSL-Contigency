# Question 1

### What is the primary objective of Quality Assurance in manufacturing?

- A. Increase electricity consumption
- B. Ensure products meet quality standards
- C. Increase production speed only
- D. Reduce employee training

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Quality Assurance ensures that products meet customer specifications, industry standards, and company quality requirements.

---

# Question 2

### Quality checks are performed during which stage of manufacturing?

- A. Only after Dispatch
- B. Only before production
- C. Throughout the manufacturing process
- D. Only during Sales

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:**

Quality inspections are carried out from raw material procurement to the final product before dispatch.

---

# Question 3

### Which department is mainly responsible for product quality?

- A. Sales
- B. Human Resources
- C. Quality Assurance
- D. Finance

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:**

The Quality Assurance department monitors inspections, testing, and compliance with quality standards.

---

# Question 4

### Which of the following is a common quality inspection method?

- A. Visual Inspection
- B. Mechanical Testing
- C. Chemical Analysis
- D. All of the Above

**Correct Answer:** D

**Explanation:**

Quality Assurance uses multiple inspection and testing methods to verify product quality.

---

# Question 5

### Why is traceability important in manufacturing?

- A. To increase warehouse size
- B. To track products throughout production
- C. To reduce production speed
- D. To improve office management

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Traceability helps identify the origin and manufacturing history of every product.

---

# Question 6

### Which document confirms that a product meets quality specifications?

- A. Salary Slip
- B. Material Test Certificate (MTC)
- C. Attendance Register
- D. Visitor Pass

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

A Material Test Certificate provides information about the material's chemical and mechanical properties.

---

# Question 7

### Which of the following can be considered a quality defect?

- A. Surface Scratch
- B. Crack
- C. Thickness Variation
- D. All of the Above

**Correct Answer:** D

**Explanation:**

Any deviation from specified quality standards is treated as a defect and investigated.

---

# Question 8

### Why are laboratory tests conducted?

- A. To decorate the laboratory
- B. To verify material properties
- C. To package products
- D. To transport products

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

Laboratory tests confirm that the material meets required chemical, mechanical, and physical specifications.

---

# Question 9

### What is one benefit of continuous quality improvement?

- A. Better product consistency
- B. Reduced customer satisfaction
- C. Increased product defects
- D. Delayed production

**Correct Answer:** A

**Explanation:**

Continuous improvement helps reduce defects, improve efficiency, and increase customer satisfaction.

---

# Question 10

### Why is Quality Assurance important?

- A. It improves customer confidence
- B. It reduces manufacturing defects
- C. It supports compliance with standards
- D. All of the Above

**Correct Answer:** D

**Explanation:**

Quality Assurance ensures reliable products, customer satisfaction, and continuous improvement across manufacturing operations.
